Links

  • Sheezy.art - Very cool DeviantArt alternative! Does not allow AI and NFT content. Based on the classic art gallery site Sheezyart.com (with approval from the developers of the original site). Check it out!
  • Fontworks Font Collection - Free download collection of (almost?) all fonts by Fontworks, a Japanese foundry known for having their fonts featured in carious video games, notably Nintendo. You can also download fonts individually! (My favorite fonts are Yuruka and Pop Happiness btw)
  • Dynacomware Font Collection - Similar to Fontworks Font Collection, but with Dynacomware, another well known foundry. You can't download fonts individually though, unless you brought a torrenting software with you. (My favorites are DF Marumoji and DF Craft Yu)
  • Asterism - A site owned by Satsuyako where you download some cute fonts! You can also download web graphics (like favicons and backgrounds) from here. Some of Asterism's fonts (like Cherry Bomb and Cherish) are also featured in ibisPaintX app.
  • Daniwell's site - You read it right, that's the site of Vocaloid producer Daniwell. This site has super cool interactive web toys and games! One of his projects, Wonder of Wonder Art, blew up in popularity 4 years ago and I remember seeing posts and videos of it on Twitter. I tried it myself and it was awesome!
  • Minecraft Plus - Want a cool screensaver? I recommend Minecraft Plus. I don't use it anymore for computer-saving purposes, but the screensavers are super cool.
  • Simple Sticky Notes - Do you use Windows 10/11, but miss the classic sticky notes from Windows 7/don't like the current Sticky Notes? Here you go! It has cool themes as well :)
  • Miru - Download this and you can watch anime absolutely for FREE! With no ads, malware and bullshit. You can also link your AniList here, therefore there will be no way to forget to update your anime progress. (It's also open source, which makes it GOATed!)
  • Krita - Never pay for a cool art program again! It is a free and open source program where you draw and stuff. I use it since September 2022 and now I can't go back. Please keep in mind that there might be more bugs than your previous art program, as open-source programs tend to have more bugs than closed-source.(Donate Krita team here!)
